Hello! This is veekun, my personal site and a focal point for software I write.

The biggest and oldest component is the Pokédex, which I've been working on for some time now. It's mostly focused on displaying and examining data, because I'm a nerd and that's the sort of thing I find interesting, but I'm gradually adding more textual descriptions of more practical use.

A lot of people consider this to be a Pokémon fansite, since it only hosted the Pokédex for quite some time, but that was never my intent. veekun is meant to host any and all Web gizmos I write; the Pokédex just happens to be the most involved.

I believe very strongly in sharing code. In that spirit, the whole site is open source, all the data and sprites are freely available, and I try to be open about what I'm working on. I love to hear what I'm doing wrong or right; there's a sporadically-used bug tracker to complain in, or you can come bug me on IRC.

The site is currently written in Python, uses PostgreSQL as a data store, and runs on an Ubuntu box.

The name doesn't mean anything in particular. I always write it in lowercase, but almost everyone else capitalizes it. Yes, I know "veekun" is bogus Japanese.

And, of course, I couldn't have done this alone.

Contrary to popular belief, I go by "Eevee" and have never used "veekun" as a handle.

I'm just some guy who loves hacking; been at it for over a decade now. Usability and user interface design are fascinating, too, but those are more social arts and I'm still quite the amateur.

I'm extremely picky about software, whether it's my own or someone else's, and tend to rave about bad products a bit too much. After having been bitten by it too many times, I can't stand sloppy languages or sloppy databases. I think open source is fantastic and important.

I live in western Washington and work from home doing Web development in Perl.
